Our Story

Where Science
Meets the Sea

Synet is a bilingual ocean science publication born from a simple belief: the ocean is the most important thing on Earth, and most people know almost nothing about it.

Our Mission

Why Synet Exists

The ocean covers 71% of our planet, produces half our oxygen, absorbs a third of our carbon, and feeds billions of people. Yet ocean literacy remains dangerously low. Synet bridges the gap between peer-reviewed science and public understanding — in two languages.

Science, Translated

We take complex peer-reviewed research and translate it into readable, accurate stories — without dumbing it down. Ocean science deserves serious treatment and wide reach.

Bilingual by Design

Every article on Synet is fully available in both Indonesian and English. Ocean science shouldn't have a language barrier — especially for the country with the world's longest coastline.

Conservation-Driven

We believe that understanding the ocean is the first step to protecting it. Every story we publish is chosen for its relevance to ocean health and its power to change how readers see our blue planet.

What We Stand For

Our Editorial Principles

01

Accuracy First

Every claim is sourced to peer-reviewed research or established scientific institutions. We correct errors promptly and transparently.

02

No Sensationalism

The ocean is extraordinary enough without exaggeration. We present facts clearly and let the science speak — even when it's complicated or uncomfortable.

03

Open & Free

All Synet content is free to read, forever. Knowledge about our shared ocean should not sit behind a paywall.
